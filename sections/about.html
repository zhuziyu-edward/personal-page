About Me

I am Ziyu Zhu (诸子钰), a fifth-year Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science and Technology at Tsinghua University. My research lies at the intersection of 3D vision, language understanding, and multimodal learning—specifically focusing on 3D Vision-Language Models, Multimodal Large Language Models, and Spatial Understanding.

I have authored multiple top-tier conference papers and have led or contributed to several research projects dedicated to enabling intelligent agents to perceive, reason about, and interact with the 3D physical world. My representative works include 3D-VisTA (developed at BIGAI) and Seed1.5VL (developed at ByteDance Seed), which advance the state of the art in 3D scene understanding and multimodal reasoning.
